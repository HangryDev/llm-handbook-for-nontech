# 2. The Pilot Is Free — What This Means for the Agreement

The March 28 pilot has no ticket revenue.
There is nothing to split for that event.

However, the agreement still matters for the pilot, for three reasons:

**A. Everyone is contributing unpaid time.**
The agreement should acknowledge this explicitly so there are no hidden expectations of compensation for the pilot.

**B. The pilot is a measurement.**
It is the first real test of each partner's contribution — especially distribution.
지현 and 혜린's audiences have not been tested in action yet.
How many people actually show up through their channels will directly inform the Cohort 2 split.

**C. Assets produced from the pilot belong to the platform.**
The lecture recording, slides, workshop materials, and any content created during the pilot are platform IP.
This must be agreed before the event, not after.

**Recommended framing for the pilot:**

> The March 28 pilot is a joint investment by all partners.
> No partner receives payment for the pilot.
> All assets produced become platform property.
> The pilot is the basis for calibrating the Cohort 2 agreement.

# 3. Revenue Split for Cohort 2 (First Paid Cohort)

This is the split that matters for the contract.

## Step 1 — Define net revenue first

Before any split is applied, agree on what counts as distributable revenue.

```
Gross ticket revenue
  minus  platform / course hosting fees  (est. 5–10%)
  minus  payment processing fees         (est. 2–3%)
  minus  any agreed pre-approved marketing spend
= Net distributable revenue
```

The split percentages below apply to net distributable revenue, not gross.

Example:
- Gross: 4,500,000 KRW (30 participants × 150,000 KRW)
- Platform fees: 300,000 KRW
- Processing: 100,000 KRW
- Net distributable: 4,100,000 KRW → split this amount

Marketing spend is only deducted if all partners agree to it in advance.
No one can unilaterally spend from the common pool.

Proposed split for Cohort 2:

| Person | Share | Reasoning |

---

## Why this split

**Why 한결 gets 50%, not 60%:**
The research supports 60% for a pure creator-led model with one operator partner.
But here, two partners each bring a real, separate distribution audience.
Distribution is the scarce resource in early-stage platforms.
Recognizing that with 18% each is fair and keeps 지현 and 혜린 motivated.
한결's 50% already reflects both his platform ownership and his lead instructor role.

**Why 경재 gets 15%, not more:**
경재 is assistant-level on teaching and contributes to content.
He brings no distribution and no audience.
15% is appropriate for that contribution profile.
If 경재's teaching role grows — for example, if he leads a session or develops a standalone module — this can be revised for Cohort 3.

**Why 지현 and 혜린 are nearly equal:**
Their role descriptions are identical: distribution + operations.
Their audiences are approximately the same size and separate.
The 1% difference (18% vs. 17%) is not meaningful — it is a placeholder until the pilot shows which channel actually converts better.
After the pilot, adjust this if there is a clear difference in performance.

---

## What 한결's 50% covers

This must be stated clearly in the agreement to avoid partner confusion.

한결's share covers:
- Platform infrastructure costs (course platform, community tools, domain, hosting)
- His own time as lead instructor
- His role as primary content creator
- His ongoing platform development work between cohorts

It does NOT mean 한결 takes 50% and has no costs.
The platform costs come out of his share, not before the split.
This is intentional — it keeps the split formula clean and gives 한결 incentive to keep infrastructure costs low.

# 4. Cohort-by-Cohort Review Clause

This split is for Cohort 2 only.

After each cohort, partners review:
- Did each person deliver on their stated role?
- Did distribution actually come through the claimed channels?
- Did anyone take on more or less than expected?

The split for the next cohort is then agreed based on evidence.

This protects everyone:
- Underperformers do not keep a large share by default
- Over-contributors have a clear path to a larger share
- No one is locked into a split that no longer reflects reality
