## 1. Core Business Concept

An education-centered AI community that continuously tracks AI developments and converts them into practical workflows, tools, and skills for non-technical users. Members subscribe to receive curated knowledge, ready-to-use AI workflows, and training while participating in a community where advanced members ("AI champions") can contribute and monetize AI workflows.

### Target Users

| Segment | Description |
|---|---|
| Non-technical professionals | Want to use AI in their work but lack technical background |
| Beginners | Little or no knowledge of AI |
| AI champions | Community members who build and monetize AI workflows/agents |
| Executives / Advanced professionals | Interested in high-level AI workflow training |

### Problem Being Solved

AI tools, techniques, and news evolve rapidly, making it difficult for non-technical users to identify which developments matter and how to apply them immediately in their work.

### Proposed Solution

A curated AI learning and workflow ecosystem that converts rapidly evolving AI knowledge into practical and ready-to-use assets: workflows, AI skills, tools, and educational content.

### Product Type

- Education platform
- Subscription knowledge service
- Community platform
- Workflow / tool marketplace

### Geographic Scope

- **Country:** South Korea
- **Primary City:** Seoul

## 2. Market Research

### Market Definition

AI education and AI productivity enablement market focused on helping non-technical professionals adopt AI tools and workflows.

**Target Segment:** Professional AI upskilling for non-technical knowledge workers in South Korea.

### Market Size

| Level | Estimate |
|---|---|
| TAM | Global AI education & productivity training — multi-billion USD |
| SAM | South Korean professional AI training & productivity tooling — hundreds of millions USD |
| SOM | Early-stage niche community targeting AI-curious professionals in Seoul |

> *Note: Precise segmentation data limited; market rapidly evolving.*

### Growth & Trends

**Growth Rate:** High — generative AI adoption driving estimated double-digit annual growth.

**Key Trends:**
- Rapid adoption of generative AI tools in professional workflows
- Growing demand for AI literacy among non-technical professionals
- Shift from theoretical AI education to practical workflow training
- Rise of AI communities and creator-driven knowledge ecosystems

### Demand Drivers

- Pressure for productivity improvements
- Corporate interest in AI adoption
- Fear of being left behind in AI transformation

### Customer Behavior Patterns

- Learning through online courses and communities
- Strong reliance on curated learning due to information overload
- Preference for practical examples over academic content

### Barriers to Entry

- Need for continuous content updates
- Founder credibility and expertise
- Community building difficulty

### Market Risks

- Rapid platform changes
- Education commoditization
- Competition from global platforms

### Market Opportunities

- Localized AI education
- Community-driven knowledge platforms
- Workflow-focused AI productivity training
