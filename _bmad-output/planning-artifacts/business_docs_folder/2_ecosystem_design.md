## Ecosystem Design Document

Version: Draft v3  
Date: March 2026

---

# 1. Purpose of the Ecosystem

The ecosystem exists to develop and surface **AI Power Users** who design practical **AI systems** for real work.

The goal is not simply to teach AI tools.

The goal is to produce:

- real AI systems
- applied AI architectures
- agentic systems
- practical knowledge about AI operations

These outputs are then transformed into consumable formats for the broader community.

---

# 2. Core Principles

### Applied AI First

The ecosystem prioritizes real systems over theoretical discussion.

The focus is on **AI systems, automation architectures, and operational use of AI.**

---

### Expertise Over Participation

Status within the ecosystem is determined by demonstrated expertise and impact.

---

### Small Elite Core

The ecosystem maintains a relatively small group of highly capable AI Champions.

Target size:

20–50 Champions.

This ensures:

- high quality outputs
- strong reputation
- high signal-to-noise ratio

---

### Structured Knowledge Production

Knowledge produced by Champions is structured and distributed by the platform.

Operators help convert raw expert insights into usable knowledge.

---

# 3. Ecosystem Roles

The ecosystem contains four primary roles.

---

## Founder

Responsibilities

- define long-term vision
- guide ecosystem strategy
- identify emerging AI opportunities
- select and develop early Champions

---

## Operators (Company Team)

Operators belong to the company and manage the knowledge production pipeline.

Responsibilities include:

- interviewing Champions
- extracting practical knowledge
- structuring AI systems
- producing educational materials
- organizing workshops and programs
- managing the community

Operators function similarly to:

- journalists documenting expert insights
- graduate students preparing lectures based on expert research

Their role is to convert expert knowledge into formats that others can learn from.

---

## AI Champions

AI Champions are domain experts who design advanced **AI systems**.

Their primary focus is **building high-impact AI systems and publishing applied knowledge.**

Typical outputs include:

- agentic systems
- domain-specific AI automation
- applied AI architectures
- case studies of real AI usage
- tool experimentation results

Champions focus on **building and maintaining systems and generating expertise**, not managing the community.

Champions may occasionally teach lectures or workshops, but this is optional.

Ultimate goal is to onboard them as champion/operator - highest on the counsil.

---

## Members

Members are professionals who join to learn practical AI usage.

Typical activities include:

- attending workshops
- learning from published AI systems
- applying AI in their work
- participating in discussions

Members may eventually produce valuable systems that lead to Champion recognition.

---

# 4. Champion Archetypes

Champions typically fall into several archetypes.

---

### Automation Architect

Designs complex AI automation systems.

Examples

- research pipelines
- internal AI tools
- multi-step automation systems

---

### Tool Explorer

Explores emerging AI tools and system capabilities.

Examples

- tool comparisons
- experimentation
- system benchmarking

---

### Systems Designer

Designs AI systems for real operational environments.

Examples

- consulting systems
- content production systems
- operational AI architectures

---

### Domain Specialist

Applies AI deeply within a specific industry.

Examples

- legal AI systems
- marketing automation systems
- research AI systems

---

# 5. Champion Pipeline

The ecosystem develops expertise through several stages.

Member  
↓  
Power User  
↓  
Champion

---

## Member

Learns basic AI tools and systems.

Typical activities

- attending workshops
- studying AI systems
- experimenting with AI in their work

---

## Power User

Begins experimenting and building small AI systems.

Typical activities

- modifying AI systems
- experimenting with tools
- sharing discoveries

Some Power Users may produce original AI systems or case studies.

---

## Champion

Champions are individuals whose work demonstrates significant impact.

Promotion to Champion status is determined by the company based on:

- impact of published knowledge
- quality of designed AI systems
- originality of agentic architectures
- real-world application value

Champion status is not based on activity level or participation.

---

# 6. Knowledge Production Model

Knowledge creation follows a structured pipeline.

Champion insight  
↓

↓  
AI system documentation  
↓  
Educational content  
↓

This system ensures expert insights become usable knowledge.

# 7. AI Systems Publishing

Champions produce original AI systems or case studies.

Operators work with Champions to convert these into:

- documented AI systems
- guides
- lectures
- workshop materials

Approved systems become part of the official **AI Systems Library**.

# 10. Knowledge System

The ecosystem accumulates structured knowledge about applied AI systems.

This includes:

- AI systems
- tools
- techniques
- architectures
- case studies

Over time this becomes a structured **knowledge graph of applied AI systems.**

---

# 11. Community Culture

The ecosystem promotes a culture of:

- experimentation
- practical problem solving
- intellectual curiosity
- open sharing of discoveries

The focus is on **building and evolving AI systems**, not status or discussion.

---

# 12. Long-Term Evolution

Over time the ecosystem becomes a network of highly capable AI power users.

Members gain:

- practical expertise
- reputation within the ecosystem
- collaboration opportunities
- economic participation

The long-term goal is to build a professional network that shapes how **AI systems are designed and applied across industries.**
