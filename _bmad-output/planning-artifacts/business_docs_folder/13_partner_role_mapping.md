All revenue splits are derived from these four functions.

# Overlap and Concentration Risks

Two things to note before moving to the split numbers.

### 1. 지현 and 혜린 have identical roles

Both are handling distribution + operations.
This is fine — two people sharing a function is normal.
But when assigning the split, their combined allocation should be set first, then divided between them.

Do not assign each of them the same share you would give a single person doing the role.
The function has one weight; they split that weight between them.

### 2. 경재 has no distribution

경재 brings content and teaching — which are high-value — but no audience or promotion capability.
This means the platform's growth for the pilot is almost entirely dependent on:
- 한결's personal network
- 지현 and 혜린's audiences (~100 each)

This is a normal early-stage situation.
It does mean distribution is the scarce resource at this stage, which is relevant to how it is weighted in the split.

# Open Questions Before Moving to Step 2 (Split Numbers)

These need answers before the actual percentages can be set fairly.

1. **Who is the primary teacher on March 28?**
   Is 한결 the lead instructor with 경재 supporting, or are they equal co-instructors?
   This affects how Content + Teaching weight is divided between them.

2. **Are 지현 and 혜린's audiences overlapping or separate?**
   ~100 each could mean ~200 total, or it could mean many of the same people.
   If they largely overlap, their combined distribution value is lower than it appears.

3. **Does 경재 bring any audience or network?**
   Even a small one matters. If 경재 does any promotion, that should be noted.

4. **Is 혜린's physical space being used for the March 28 pilot specifically?**
   If yes, it reduces costs and that should be recognized in the agreement.
   If the pilot is online-only, this contribution comes into play later.

5. **What is the expected ticket price / revenue for the pilot?**
   The split percentages do not change based on this, but agreeing on the number in advance avoids surprises when it is time to distribute.
