# 1. Purpose

This document defines how revenue and incentives are distributed within the ecosystem.

It serves two purposes:

1. A **partner agreement reference** — what each person earns and why
2. A **job reference table** — what each role earns across different program types, for use when new programs or new people are brought in

The platform follows a **founder-led model with program-based revenue sharing**.

Goals:
- reward knowledge creators
- sustain the platform
- support operators
- keep ownership clear

---

# 2. Core Principle

The platform follows a simple structural rule.

**Founder owns the platform.**
**Collaborators earn from the programs they participate in.**

The platform itself is a long-term asset owned by the founder and company.

Revenue generated from specific programs is shared with collaborators involved in those programs.

This prevents ownership fragmentation while still rewarding contribution.

# 5. Role Definitions

All revenue splits are derived from functional roles.
People are assigned to roles — not the other way around.

| Role | Function | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Lead Instructor | Content + Teaching | Primary knowledge creator and main teacher. Owns curriculum IP. |
| Co-Instructor | Content + Teaching (support) | Assists with content creation and supports delivery. |
| Distribution + Operations | Distribution + Operations | Brings audience, handles promotion, manages logistics and community. |
| Platform | Infrastructure + Strategy | Owns and maintains the platform, tools, and long-term ecosystem. |

## 7D. External Platform Corporate Training

When a third-party training platform sources the corporate client and you deliver the training through their platform.

This is the **early cash flow model** — available before the platform has its own corporate sales pipeline.

How it works:
- The external platform handles marketing, sales, and client acquisition
- You deliver the training content
- The external platform takes their cut (typically 20–40%)
- You keep the remainder

| Role                            |                   Share | Notes                                       |
| ------------------------------- | ----------------------: | ------------------------------------------- |
| Instructor (한결)                 |                  60–80% | After the external platform takes their cut |
| Co-Instructor (경재, if involved) | Agreed directly with 한결 | Typically 20–30% of 한결's share              |

**Partner split note:**
This revenue stream is NOT subject to the 4-partner split.
지현 and 혜린 do not contribute to sourcing or delivering external platform training.
Revenue from this track is split only between the delivering instructors.

**Content ownership:**
All training materials, slides, and recordings created for external sessions become platform assets.
They may be repurposed for the platform's own programs.

**Strategic purpose:**
- Generates cash flow before the platform's own B2B pipeline is established
- Builds credibility and case studies for direct enterprise training later
- Creates content that feeds the AI Systems Library

# 8. Cohort Review Process

Splits are set **per cohort**, not permanently.

After each cohort, partners review:

- Did each person deliver on their stated role?
- Did distribution channels convert as expected?
- Did anyone contribute significantly more or less than agreed?

The split for the next cohort is agreed based on this review.

This protects everyone:
- Underperformers do not carry a large share by default
- Over-contributors have a clear path to a larger share
- Roles can evolve without renegotiating permanent terms

**Scheduled review points:**

| Review | Timing |
|---|---|
| Cohort 1 → Cohort 2 split | June 2026 (after pilot learnings) |
| Cohort 2 → Cohort 3 split | After Cohort 2 closes |

# 12. Why Equity Is Limited Early

In early-stage ecosystems with part-time collaborators, distributing equity too early creates problems.

Risks:
- inactive co-founders holding a permanent share
- misaligned incentives as roles evolve
- ownership fragmentation making decisions slow
- complexity that is hard to undo

The program revenue sharing model rewards real, current contribution without permanent ownership commitments.

Equity eligibility opens only after sustained, demonstrated long-term impact.

# 13. Long-Term Revenue Model

As the ecosystem grows, additional revenue streams emerge.

| Stage | Revenue source |
|---|---|
| Now (March–June) | Workshops, cohort programs |
| Early (April onward) | External platform corporate training — parallel cash flow, no own B2B pipeline needed |
| Mid-term (July–September) | Online courses, paid community subscription, low-ticket digital products |
| Mid-term (September+) | Direct enterprise training — once external sessions build credibility |
| Long-term | AI systems marketplace, advanced Champion programs, executive programs |

Knowledge production loop:

```
Champion builds AI system
  ↓
System documented and published
  ↓
Users learn or apply the system
  ↓
Champion earns through courses or system breakdowns
```

This creates a compounding knowledge-driven revenue ecosystem.

# 14. Incentive Model Summary

| Layer | Structure |
|---|---|
| Platform ownership | Held by founder (80–90%) |
| Program revenue | Shared among contributors per cohort |
| Operational support | Fixed fees or small revenue share |
| Future equity | Reserved for proven long-term contributors |

This structure keeps incentives aligned, rewards current contribution, and protects the platform's long-term integrity.
