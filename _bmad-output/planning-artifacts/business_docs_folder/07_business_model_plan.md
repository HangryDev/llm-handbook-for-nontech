# 2. Business Model

## Core model

The business is a **creator-led AI education business** built on applied AI systems knowledge.

The creator (한결) is the primary intellectual asset.
Everything else is distribution, support, and scale.

## The funnel

| Stage | Format | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Discovery | YouTube, social content | Attract audience, demonstrate expertise |
| Trust | Newsletter | Build owned relationship, deepen authority |
| First purchase | Low-ticket digital product | Validate that strangers pay for your knowledge |
| Core offer | Cohort workshop / course | Primary revenue, validate teaching format |
| Retention | Paid community | Recurring revenue, implementation support |
| High-ticket | Consulting / mastermind / B2B | Highest margin, deepest engagement |

## What this business is NOT in Phase 1

- Not a community platform
- Not a Champion network
- Not a multi-instructor operation

Those come later.
Phase 1 is a solopreneur content and education business.
It scales in later phases.

# 3. The Content Engine

The content engine is the foundation.
Without it, there is no audience. Without an audience, there is no business.

## Primary channels

### YouTube
The main discovery channel.

Content types:
- AI system walkthroughs
- Tool comparisons for practitioners
- Workflow demonstrations
- "How I use AI for X" breakdowns

Publishing rhythm: 1–2 videos per week.

The goal is not views. The goal is **trust from the right people** — professionals who want practical AI in their work.

### Newsletter
The primary owned asset.

YouTube drives discovery. The newsletter converts viewers into a relationship the platform owns.

Content format: weekly. One focused insight, one AI system, one practical application.

The newsletter is the bridge between free content and paid products.
It is a platform asset — it belongs to the business, not to any partner.

## What the content engine proves

Before spending money on ads or community infrastructure, the content engine answers:

- Do people find this content useful?
- Do they subscribe and stay subscribed?
- Do they click on offers?

Without these answers, there is nothing worth building a community around.

# 4. Offer Ladder

The offer ladder is built incrementally — each stage is only added after the previous one is validated.

```
Free tier
  YouTube content
  Newsletter

Low-ticket (15,000–49,000 KRW)
  AI Systems playbook or guide
  Workshop recording
  System template pack

Core offer (150,000–300,000 KRW)
  Live cohort workshop
  Hands-on AI systems course

B2B parallel track (platform-dependent pricing)
  Corporate training through external platforms
  No own sales pipeline required at this stage

Mid-ticket future (300,000–1,000,000 KRW / month or year)
  Paid community membership
  Access to AI systems library, live sessions, expert network

High-ticket future (project-based)
  Direct consulting
  Enterprise training (no intermediary)
  Mastermind / advanced programs
```

**The rule: do not build the next stage until the current stage is validated.**

A validated stage means: strangers (not just friends and network) are paying for it.

# 6. Phase 2 — First Products
### July – September 2026 (90–180 days)

**Goal: Validate that strangers pay for your knowledge.**

At the end of this phase the question to answer is:
*Can I generate revenue from people who found me through content, not through personal relationships?*

---

## July

### Low-ticket digital product launch
The first paid offer.

Format options:
- PDF guide: a structured walkthrough of one AI system
- Template pack: prompt templates, workflow diagrams, system components
- Workshop recording: the March 28 pilot, edited and packaged

Price: 15,000–49,000 KRW.

This is not about revenue.
It is about **proof that the content engine converts**.
If 20–30 people buy this from a list of 300–500, the model is working.

### Cohort 2 (first paid workshop)
The second workshop, now paid.

Changes from the pilot:
- Paid entry (validates willingness to pay)
- Recruit beyond founder network — community, YouTube, newsletter
- Apply partner split agreed in June

Target: 20–40 paying participants.

Distribution responsibility activates here.
지현 and 혜린's audiences matter now — there is something to promote.

### External training continues
Running as a regular parallel stream.

---

## August

### Community infrastructure — light version only
If the first cohort showed demand for ongoing support, set up a lightweight community space.

**This is not a paid community yet.**
It is a free implementation layer for cohort participants.
Purpose: keep the learning going, surface Power Users, test what members actually want from a community.

The decision of whether to make it paid comes in September, based on observed behavior.

### Newsletter monetization begins
With 300–700 subscribers and consistent open rates:
- Consider a sponsor slot in the newsletter
- Consider affiliate recommendations for AI tools used in the systems

This is supplemental income, not primary.

---

## September

### Six-month review
Full review of the first six months:

- What revenue came from where?
- Which partner contributed what?
- Did external training justify the effort?
- What does the audience actually want next?

### Community decision
Based on August behavior:

**Option A — Community as free implementation layer**
Keep community free. Revenue comes from courses and products.
Community is a retention and Power User discovery tool.

**Option B — Community as paid product**
Charge monthly or annually.
Requires: consistent new AI systems, live sessions, expert access, ongoing value.
Only choose this if members are actively using the free version and asking for more.

### Partner structure review
- Was the 4-partner model the right shape?
- Do all partners want to continue into Phase 3?
- Are any roles no longer needed, or has any role grown significantly?

# 7. Phase 3 — Education Business
### October 2026 – March 2027 (6–12 months)

**Goal: Regular revenue from a repeatable education product.**

The solopreneur content model has been validated.
Phase 3 is about making it reliable.

---

## What Phase 3 builds on

By this point:
- Content engine is running and compounding
- Newsletter is a real owned asset (1,000+ subscribers)
- At least one paid product has been validated
- One paid cohort has been delivered
- External training is a known cash flow source

---

## Regular cohort cadence

Run cohorts on a predictable schedule.
Target: 2–3 cohorts per year.

Each cohort:
- Applies learnings from the previous one
- Is promoted to newsletter + YouTube + partner networks
- Uses the partner split defined in `11_revenue_architecture.md`

---

## Champion program — early version

By this stage, 2–3 Champion candidates have been identified.
Begin formalizing:
- Champion-produced AI systems published in the library
- Optional Champion lectures or system breakdowns
- Revenue sharing per `11_revenue_architecture.md` Section 7F

---

## Community — activated if validated

If the September decision was Option B (paid community):
- Set pricing (target: 30,000–70,000 KRW / month)
- Define the ongoing value: new AI systems, live sessions, expert access
- Apply Stu McLaren / Jay Clouse pricing logic: founding member price → raise later

If the September decision was Option A (free implementation layer):
- Keep community free but structured
- Use it to discover Champions and Power Users
- Revisit paid community when there are 100+ active free members

---

## B2B direct training begins

With credibility from external platform sessions and a recognized content presence:
- Begin pursuing direct corporate training without a platform intermediary
- Higher revenue per engagement
- Applies the direct enterprise split from `11_revenue_architecture.md` Section 7E

# 8. Phase 4 — Network and Scale
### April 2027 onward (Year 2+)

**Goal: Transition from solopreneur education business to a professional network.**

This is where the original ecosystem vision — AI Champions, AI Power Users, the AI Professional Network — fully activates.

It is not the starting point. It is the reward for executing Phases 1–3 correctly.

---

## What changes in Phase 4

The shift from solopreneur to platform:

| Phase 1–3 | Phase 4+ |
|---|---|
| 한결 is the primary content creator | Champions co-produce knowledge |
| One instructor per cohort | Multiple expert-led programs |
| Community is free or early paid | Community is a recognized professional network |
| B2B through external platforms | Direct enterprise sales pipeline |
| Solo content engine | Team-supported production |

---

## Long-term vision activated

From `1_vision_strategy.md`:

> The long-term vision is to build an AI Professional Network where members learn AI, build systems, share workflows, develop reputation, and create economic value.

This is the outcome of Phase 4.

It is built on:
- An audience that trusts you (built in Phase 1)
- Products that prove your value (built in Phase 2)
- A repeatable education business (built in Phase 3)
- A community that earned its existence through real demand (activated in Phase 3–4)

# 9. Partner Roles Across Phases

| Phase               | 한결                                               | 경재                                                 | 지현 & 혜린                                                                       |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Phase 1 (content)   | Primary creator, lead instructor, content engine | Co-creates content, assists in workshop delivery   | Distribution + ops activate for the free pilot; main value unlocks in Phase 2 |
| Phase 2 (products)  | Leads all product creation and cohort delivery   | Co-teaches cohorts, contributes to product content | Distribute paid cohort, manage community infrastructure                       |
| Phase 3 (education) | Platform leader, curriculum, strategy            | Champion-level contributor if trajectory holds     | Community management, operations, ongoing promotion                           |
| Phase 4 (network)   | Platform founder, ecosystem architect            | Potential Champion or operator role                | Core operators of a scaled platform                                           |

**The key shift for 지현 and 혜린:**
Their distribution value is most powerful when there is something concrete to promote — a paid cohort, a digital product, a community membership.
In Phase 1 the pilot is free, so the primary measure of their value is bringing warm bodies who convert to email subscribers.
In Phase 2, the measure becomes paying customers.
